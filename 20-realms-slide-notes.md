# 20-Realms Slide Notes

Source: Google Slides deck titled "20-Realms"
Slides captured: 56

## High-Level Summary

20-Realms is presented as a free-to-play, cross-platform, top-down 3D tabletop RPG app for D&D-style campaigns. It targets both players and Dungeon Masters, emphasizing remote play, deep customization, flexible camera options, world building, survival systems, homebrew creation, and community-made content. Planned platforms include iOS, Android, Steam/PC, with console listed as a possibility.

### Slide 3 - What Is 20-Realms?
- A top-down, 3D tabletop RPG app for playing D&D-style campaigns online.
- Designed so groups can keep playing even when they cannot meet in person.
- Deeply customizable and open to everyone.
- Free to the public across iOS, Android, Steam/PC, and possibly console.

### Slide 4 - Core Pillars
- Accessible: free-to-play, cross-platform, easy to pick up.
- Customizable: worlds, characters, rules, and content can be shaped by users.
- Social: built for remote groups to play together smoothly.
- Creative: the Lab lets users build and share content.
- Immersive: flexible camera and rich survival systems.

### Slide 5 - For Players
- Large character customization options.
- Full 3D character maker.
- Players can choose camera/view/pace preferences.
- Players can enter community-made worlds and homebrew content.

### Slide 6 - For Dungeon Masters
- DM freedom to customize maps, players, and encounters.
- World-building tools are intended to be powerful and easy to use.
- Campaign systems can be toggled on/off, including hunger, sanity, and more.

### Slide 8 - Section: Camera & View

### Slide 9 - Camera System
- Base experience is top-down 3D for players and DM.
- Mobile performance option: 2D top-down mode.
- 2D mode must be set in menu settings before play begins.
- Immersion option: third-person view following the character.
- Players choose the view that fits their device and playstyle.

### Slide 10 - Section: Building the World
- Covers how DMs create campaigns and maps.

### Slide 11 - Creating a Campaign
- One or multiple DMs can create or join a campaign.
- Campaigns start from a base setup.
- Three core creation tools: Paint tool, Foliage tool, Building tool.

### Slide 12 - Creation Tools
- Foliage Tool places trees, bushes, grass, flowers, and more.
- Paint Tool decorates ground with paths, water, grass, mud, lava, and more.
- Building Tool uses premade buildings for quick towns and cities.
- Modular Builder allows custom structures piece by piece.
- DM can toggle snapping when building points of interest.
- Design goal: easy for beginners, deep for advanced DMs.

### Slide 16 - Section: Environment & Simulation
- Covers living/reactive terrain and liquids.

### Slide 17 - Water System
- Water can be placed via Paint tool or dedicated liquid tool.
- Water fills gaps/holes naturally.
- Boats allow sailing/travel using joystick navigation.
- On water, click-to-move is replaced by joystick for smoother control.
- Players can swim and dive for underwater ruins and loot.
- No flooding for performance: water flows to nearest dip/hole and stops, or follows a set river-like flow range and stops.
- Lava and other liquids follow the same rules.

### Slide 18 - Damage & Destruction
- Effects like Fireball, floods, or lava damage destructible objects.
- Walls can break down; trees can burn and be destroyed.
- Destruction is simple, with objects popping away rather than dramatic physics.
- Boats/vehicles are not deleted; they become inoperable and can be repaired.
- A D100 result can cause temporary environmental destruction that reverts afterward.

### Slide 19 - Section: Core Gameplay
- Covers dice, movement, and combat.

### Slide 20 - Rolling Dice
- Dice are dropped onto the board for everyone to see.
- Dice physically roll until stopped, revealing the result to all players.
- Rolls determine success/failure for attacks and checks.
- Dice set includes D1/Coin, D4, D6, D8, D10, D12, D20, and D100.

### Slide 21 - Movement
- Outside combat, players move freely by walking/running.
- Vehicles use joystick on mobile or WASD on keyboard.
- In combat, movement becomes grid-locked by movement stat, such as 30 ft.
- Free movement returns after combat.
- Vehicles can be stopped by solid objects.
- Ram collisions may damage both sides, under consideration.
- Players can use unlocked emotes.

### Slide 22 - Emotes
- Up to 50 emotes planned.
- Categories include greetings, emotions, gestures, actions/poses, and flair.
- Examples: wave, bow, curtsy, salute, nod, shake head, handshake, hug, high-five, fist bump, blow kiss, wink, laugh, cry, cheer, clap, facepalm, shrug, sigh, gasp, blush, pout, rage, cower/fear, surprised, confused, point, beckon, halt, thumbs up/down, peace sign, shush, dance, sit, kneel, lie down, sleep, flex, cross arms, meditate, pray, yawn, stretch, taunt, victory pose, flourish/spin, cheers/toast, whistle, backflip.

### Slide 23 - Attack System
- Triggered when one or more parties are about to fight.
- Combatants lock into grid movement based on stats, e.g. 30 ft = 6 squares.
- Combat is turn-based and ordered by initiative.
- Players can fight with weapons, spells, tools, or anything usable in a fight.
- Winners exit combat and return to free movement.
- Dead/losing players make a new character to rejoin or become spectators.

### Slide 24 - Section: Survival Systems
- Covers tension, consequences, and downtime.

### Slide 25 - Sanity
- Applies only in dungeons and caves.
- Party injuries reduce sanity.
- Untreated sanity loss can cause insanity or loss of control.
- Sanity is restored by medicine, leaving the area, or resting.

### Slide 26 - Sickness & Injuries
- Sickness can occur from rain exposure by percentage chance.
- Spreadable sickness can pass from infected enemies.
- Cured with healing potions or by visiting a doctor.
- Combat injuries can affect specific body parts.
- Example: broken leg can cut movement speed in half.

### Slide 27 - Food, Hunger & Thirst
- Food and thirst can be toggled by the DM.
- Food and drink prevent hunger/thirst harm or death.
- Neglect reduces movement range and combat performance.

### Slide 28 - Camp Features
- Players can find a spot and press camp button.
- Tents, campfire, camp flag, and gear appear with build animation.
- Camp allows short/long rests for healing and recovery.
- Clicking the flag takes the camp down.

### Slide 29 - Section: Dungeons & Caves
- Covers high-tension, immersive spaces.

### Slide 30 - Dungeons
- Premade dungeons and caves can be placed instantly by DMs.
- DMs can also custom-build dungeons and caves.
- Camera shifts toward third-person inside for immersion/tension.
- Outdoor dungeons/caves can keep top-down view if DM chooses.

### Slide 32 - Character Creation

- Players set stats, features, and backgrounds.

### Slide 33 - Character Sheet
- Shows equipment and tools.

### Slide 34 - Section: Progression & Economy
- Covers loot, XP, currency, and gear.

### Slide 35 - Loot System
- Loot comes from searching objects or defeating enemies.
- DMs control loot on every lootable entity.
- Custom loot tables define basic lootables.
- Players can send loot to party loot box or attempt to steal it.
- Stealing triggers Sleight of Hand check.
- Success sends item to thief inventory.
- Failure sends item to party box and shows theft warning.
- DM can disable warning or reveal who is stealing what.

### Slide 36 - XP System
- XP comes from daily, weekly, monthly, and custom quests.
- DMs choose which quests to hand out.
- XP follows a set equation to deter cheating.
- Account role determines XP rates: DM, Player, or Both.
- Role is locked for 6 months to prevent XP abuse.

### Slide 37 - XP Rates
- Total XP to reach Level 20: 81,700.
- Kill XP examples: Minion 15, Standard 50, Elite 150, Boss 500.
- Party split: each member gets 1 / party size.
- Quest XP examples: Daily about 170, Weekly about 675, Monthly about 2,300, Milestone about 5,000, 3-Month DM about 1,700.
- Character level-up: 120 XP each.
- Formula: XP_Award = round((Kills XP + Quest XP + CharLevel XP) x DifficultyMult x ModeMult x AntiFarmMult x RoleMult).
- Level curve: XP_To_Next(L) = 300 + 75L + 25L^2 for L = 1..19.
- Role multipliers: DM 0.90, Player 1.00, Both 0.95.
- Estimated time to max: Casual about 96 days, Regular about 48 days, Dedicated about 22 days.
- Natural 20 bonus: rolling a natural 20 grants +25 bonus XP, limited once per check or attack per encounter; DM-toggleable.

### Slide 38 - Currency
- Used for vendors, barter, and campaign upgrades.
- Found in lootable objects or people.
- Currency types: CP Copper Pieces, SP Silver Pieces, EP Electrum Pieces, GP Gold Pieces, PP Platinum Pieces.
- Conversion rates: 1 PP = 10 GP, 1 GP = 10 SP, 1 SP = 10 CP, 1 EP = 5 SP.

### Slide 39 - Equipment
- Equipment grants resistances, such as cold or heat resistance.
- Better armor makes enemies less likely to hit.
- Includes tools and weapons.
- Attunement limit is 3 items unless DM allows more.

### Slide 40 - Inventory
- Works like equipment management.
- Holds currency, food, miscellaneous items.
- May include a weight limit in the base game unless the DM toggles it off.

### Slide 41 - Section: DM Toolkit

### Slide 42 - DM Features & Tools

### Slide 43 - Minimap System
